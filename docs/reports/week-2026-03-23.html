💡 Weekly Patterns & Strategic Insights

1

Tuesday Dominates — Best Day by Every Metric

Tuesday Mar 24 was the standout day: 12 purchases and $2,445 in revenue — 2.7× the Saturday low and generating 27% of the entire week's gross revenue in a single day. Average order value on Tuesday was $203.75, suggesting higher-intent buyers converted on that day. If paid promotions or email sends were timed to Tuesday, this validates the approach. Consider anchoring major campaigns to Tuesday–Wednesday for next week.
2

Weekend Drop Is Sharp — Saturday Nearly Flatlines

Saturday (Mar 28) recorded just 2 purchases and $495 in revenue — the weakest day of the week. Interestingly, Sunday recovered to 8 purchases ($1,097), matching Monday's volume. The Mon–Fri pattern ($1,100–$2,445/day) vs. Saturday ($495) suggests this audience skews toward weekday active investors. Action: Either suppress paid spend on Saturdays to preserve budget for higher-converting weekdays, or test a Saturday-specific offer (e.g., weekend flash deal) to lift conversion.
3

Robinhood Partnership Is the #1 Revenue Driver This Week

robinhood.com sourced 12 purchases ($2,047 gross) via the partner_feed campaign — making it the single largest conversion source, ahead of homepage (9 purchases, $848) and all paid channels combined. The partner_feed campaign alone drove 21 purchases ($3,699). Action: Ensure the Robinhood integration is prioritized and optimized. Explore whether the same partner placement model can be replicated with sofi.com (3 purchases, $514) or expanded to new fintech partners.
4

Multi-Year Upgrades Are a Meaningful Revenue Layer

3-Year Upgrades (12 units, $2,844) and 2-Year Upgrades (3 units, $474) together contributed $3,318 — 36.7% of gross revenue — from purchases that likely started as annual plans. This upgrade attach rate is a powerful LTV multiplier. Monthly subscribers (12 units, $228) are low-value individually but represent future upgrade targets. Action: Audit whether the multi-year upgrade offer is consistently presented post-annual checkout, and A/B test upgrade CTA copy and timing.
5

UTM Hygiene Issues Are Obscuring Attribution Accuracy

Several data quality flags this week: {campaign_in_meta} is an unresolved Meta dynamic token (4 purchases, $801 — potentially misattributed). SponsorAd / sponsorad and topofsitebanner / TopOfSiteBanner are duplicate ad types due to inconsistent casing. Facebook is tracked as both "Facebook" and "fb" as separate sources. Action for next week: Audit and standardize UTM parameters across all paid placements. Fixing the Meta token issue alone could clarify up to $801 in revenue attribution.
