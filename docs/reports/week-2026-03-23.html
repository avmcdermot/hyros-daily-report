💡 Weekly Patterns & Strategic Insights

1

Tuesday Is Your Power Day — Lean Into It

Tuesday Mar 24 delivered 12 purchases and $2,445 — 27% of all weekly revenue in a single day, with an AOV of $203.75. This is significantly above the weekly AOV of $164.55, suggesting high-intent buyers transact mid-week. Consider scheduling major email sends, ad pushes, and content drops to land on Tuesday mornings to capitalize on this established pattern.
2

Saturday Crashes Volume But Preserves AOV — Address the Drop

Saturday saw only 2 purchases ($495), the worst volume day of the week. However, the AOV was $247.50 — the highest of any day — indicating that the small cohort who did convert were premium buyers (likely multi-year upgrades). The opportunity: Saturday traffic exists but isn't converting. Test a weekend-specific retargeting push or a time-limited offer to lift volume without sacrificing AOV.
3

Robinhood Partnership Is Your Highest-Volume Organic Source

robinhood.com drove 12 purchases and $2,047 this week — making it the single largest named source, ahead of even the Benzinga homepage (9 purchases, $848). Combined with sofi.com (3 purchases, $514), fintech partner feeds via partner_feed campaign are responsible for ~41% of weekly revenue. Expanding or deepening these partner integrations should be a near-term priority.
4

Multi-Year Upgrades Are a Meaningful Revenue Engine — Optimize the Flow

3-Year and 2-Year upgrades generated $3,318 combined (36.6% of gross revenue) across only 15 units — an implied upgrade AOV of $221.20. These buyers are converting alongside their annual plan in a single session. Test presenting the 3-Year upgrade offer earlier in the post-purchase flow, or A/B a checkout upsell nudge on the edge-4 and edge-5 pages, which already account for 71% of funnel entries.
5

UTM Hygiene Issues Are Masking True Attribution — Fix Before Next Week

Three UTM inconsistencies detected this week: (1) "Facebook" vs "fb" as utm_source — both appear as separate entries but are likely the same paid social channel; (2) "SponsorAd" vs "sponsorad" — capitalization mismatch creating duplicate rows; (3) "{campaign_in_meta}" appearing as a literal UTM value (unfired macro) for 4 purchases worth $801. These issues collectively affect ~$1,296 in revenue attribution accuracy. Standardizing UTM templates and verifying macro firing in Meta campaign settings before next week is strongly recommended.
